Sri Lankan Junglefowl

The Sri Lankan junglefowl (Gallus lafayettii), sometimes known as Lafayette's junglefowl or Ceylon junglefowl, is a bird species native to Sri Lanka. It is the national bird of the country and is closely related to the more well-known red junglefowl.

This bird is most commonly seen in scrub and woodland habitats. It may be seen readily in Yala, Sinharaja, and Kitulgala, and it lives from sea level to 2,000 meters / 6,500 feet.

Mugger Crocodile

The Mugger Crocodile (Crocodylus palustris) is the second biggest reptile in Sri Lanka. It is a medium-sized crocodilian species that lives in freshwater settings ranging from southern Iran to the Indian subcontinent and Sri Lanka.

This crocodile dwells in lakes, rivers, marshes, and manmade ponds and is an excellent swimmer. It has been listed as vulnerable to extinction since 1982, owing to habitat loss, entrapment in fishing nets, road accidents, and confrontation with people.

Purple-faced Langur

The Purple-faced Langur (Semnopithecus vetulus), also recognized as the Purple-faced Leaf Monkey, is an arboreal monkey native to Sri Lanka, characterized by its lengthy tail.

Once prevalent in the surroundings of Colombo and the wet zones of the country, its population has experienced a sharp decline, primarily attributed to the expanding urbanization. Among locals, it is familiarly referred to as the "Sri Lankan black monkey."
